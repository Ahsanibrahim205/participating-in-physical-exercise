Why physical exercise can improve
your wellness

  • Both physical and mental wellbeing have been shown to improve as a consequence of building some form of regular physical exercise into our lives.
  • Appropriate exercise can provide a ‘time out’ from otherwise stressful lives and alleviate negative or discomforting mood states.
  • It can also provide benefits such as feeling physically fit and having fun.
  • It is recognised in professional circles that, in contrast to medical remedies, like stopping smoking, reducing alcohol, changing poor eating habits or prescribing mood enhancing drugs, physical exercise can provide an rewarding experience to the participant with little risk of undesirable side-effects.
  • There are social benefits from involvement in exercise as well as the aesthetic feeling associated with movement.
  • The immediate impact of regular well-chosen exercise participation can be quite extensive, including
    1. having fun
    2. making friends
    3. getting a good physical feeling
    4. satisfaction in achieving goals
    5. becoming more confident and competent
    6. physically experiencing a sense of relaxation
    7. having extra vigour and mood elevation.
  • As time goes by and regular exercise is continued further benefits will follow. The impact will be witnessed in by amongst other things
    1. a reduction in body weight
    2. an increase in fitness, e.g. jogging speed
    3. a reduction in heart rate to complete a given task
    4. improved stamina.
  • Remember taking some exercise is normal for humans and, once started all, these benefits can help you to keep on going with your chosen physical activity.
  • Two main ways you can become physically more active are:
    1. build more of it into your regular lifestyle by taking the exercise option whenever you can and
    2. build into your life planned and structured exercise sessions.
